The Roman Empire

The Roman Empire was one of the most powerful civilizations in history, lasting from 27 BCE to 476 CE in the West and 1453 CE in the East. It was known for its military strength, architectural achievements, and vast territory that stretched across Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

Key Achievements

  • Advanced road networks, including the famous Appian Way
  • Impressive architecture such as the Colosseum and aqueducts
  • A sophisticated legal system that influenced modern laws
  • Latin language and its impact on Romance languages

Famous Emperors

  • Augustus (First Emperor)
  • Trajan (Expanded the empire to its greatest extent)
  • Marcus Aurelius (Philosopher-king)
  • Constantine the Great (First Christian emperor)
